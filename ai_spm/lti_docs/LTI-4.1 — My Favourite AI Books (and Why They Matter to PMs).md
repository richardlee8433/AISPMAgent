My favourite AI books: hashtag#LinkedInNewsEurope

In the past few months, I’ve been working more closely with LLM workflows, including RAG, evaluation, and the product decisions that surround them.
 
I found myself looking for resources that explain why certain approaches work in practice and how to make better decisions as an AI PM.
 
These three books helped clarify many of those questions.

• Hands-On Large Language Models
Clear explanations of RAG design choices, prompt patterns, and evaluation loops.
Many of the concepts map directly to the challenges I encounter when shaping LLM behaviour in product environments.

• The AI Product Manager’s Handbook
Useful for the strategic side of AI.
It covers how to define the actual problem, assess error costs, decide when AI is appropriate, and design for trust and uncertainty.

• Power and Prediction
A good reminder that AI is fundamentally “cheap prediction”, and that meaningful value often comes from rethinking workflows rather than adding complexity.

It would be great to hear what helped others deepen their understanding this year.
