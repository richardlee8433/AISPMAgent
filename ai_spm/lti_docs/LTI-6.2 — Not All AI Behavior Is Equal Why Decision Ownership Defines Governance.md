Beyond Pass/Fail: Lessons from Risk-Tiered AI Evaluation

Not all AI behavior is equal — and not all decisions are created equal.  
  
When AI knowledge starts influencing actions, who should be responsible?  
  
Most discussions focus on outputs:  
- Model accuracy  
- Hallucinations  
- Prompt quality  
  
But outputs are not the real question.  
Actions have consequences.  
Once there are consequences, there must be a responsible decision owner.  
  
Without a clear decision owner:  
• Governance becomes hindsight justification.  
• Evaluation alone is insufficient.  
• Teams may debate endlessly, even if the AI is “working as expected.”  
  
🎯 Key Takeaways  
1️⃣ Action ≠ Output  
- AI behavior = consequences  
2️⃣ Responsibility must be explicit  
- Decision ownership sits with the PM / accountable individual  
3️⃣ Evaluation as input, not just QA  
- Risk-tiered evaluation feeds governance, not just a score  
  
This is the foundation for all Judgement-as-a-Service posts:  
> Can a third party read your outputs and summarize your decision in one sentence?  
> If yes, you are now operating as a decision owner, not just a builder.
